## When to use
Use this framework when you need to evaluate or shape a strategy for a business, product, or initiative and want to separate real strategy from vague goals, slogans, or ambition statements. It is especially useful before finalizing a plan, roadmap, or major investment decision.

## Inputs required
Current business context, key challenge or bottleneck, market/customer evidence, major constraints, and the existing plan or strategy draft to evaluate.

## Output artifact
A refined strategy assessment (or strategy draft) structured around: Diagnosis (core challenge), Guiding Policy (approach), and Coherent Actions (aligned actions)—with weak/unsupported strategy elements removed.

## Common mistakes
Confusing goals with strategy, skipping diagnosis, using buzzwords or vision statements as “strategy,” listing unrelated initiatives, and failing to define coherent actions that address the real challenge.
